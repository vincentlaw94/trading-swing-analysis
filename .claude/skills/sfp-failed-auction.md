# Swing Failure Pattern (SFP) & Failed Auction Strategy

A comprehensive guide to trading the Swing Failure Pattern and Failed Auction setups - high probability reversal patterns used by smart money traders.

---

## Overview

The **Swing Failure Pattern (SFP)** and **Failed Auction (FA)** are powerful reversal patterns that identify when smart money is trapping retail traders. When combined, they create one of the highest probability reversal setups available.

**Origin:**
- SFP: Popularized by trader Tom Dante
- Failed Auction: Market Profile concept developed by Ray Barros

**Relationship to ICT/SMC:**
- SFP = Liquidity Sweep (same concept, different name)
- Both identify where smart money triggers retail stops before reversing

---

## Swing Failure Pattern (SFP)

### Definition

> A reversal pattern that occurs when price breaks a key swing high/low, triggers stop losses, but immediately reverses and closes back within the prior range.

### Key Characteristics

| Criteria | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| **Swing Level** | Significant swing high or low (obvious level) |
| **Break** | Price breaks through the level (sweeps liquidity) |
| **Wick** | Long wick at least 2-3x the candle body size |
| **Close** | Candle closes back within the prior range |
| **Volume** | Often accompanied by volume spike on the sweep |

### SFP Formation

**Bearish SFP (at swing high):**
```
        /\  <- Wick sweeps above high
       /  \
      /    \
     /      \
---------------- Previous High
     |
     | Displacement down
     v
```

**Bullish SFP (at swing low):**
```
     ^
     | Displacement up
     |
---------------- Previous Low
     \      /
      \    /
       \  /
        \/  <- Wick sweeps below low
```

### Why SFP Works

1. **Liquidity Grab** - Price sweeps stops above/below the level
2. **Trap** - Breakout traders get trapped on wrong side
3. **Smart Money Entry** - Institutions fill orders against triggered stops
4. **Reversal** - Price moves in opposite direction

> "Such price action often signals liquidity absorption: stop-loss orders placed by retail traders beyond the previous extremes get triggered, after which large market participants start opening positions in the opposite direction."

### SFP Success Rate

| SFP Type | Success Rate |
|----------|--------------|
| Standard SFP | 65-75% |
| Perfect SFP (clean wick, strong close) | **85-95%** |
| SFP + HTF alignment | 80-90% |
| SFP + Failed Auction | **90%+** |

## Failed Auction (FA)

### Definition

> A Failed Auction occurs when the market attempts to auction in one direction but fails to attract participation to sustain the move, quickly reversing back.

### Market Profile Context

In Market Profile terms:
- Price breaks the Initial Balance (first 60min range)
- Fails to hold outside for more than 30 minutes
- Returns back inside the Initial Balance
- Moves to the opposite side

### Key Characteristics

| Aspect | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| **Breakout** | Price breaks a key level |
| **No Follow-Through** | Volume doesn't increase on breakout |
| **Long Wicks** | Quick rejection, long wicks |
| **Quick Return** | Price returns to breakout point rapidly |
| **Opposite Move** | Price then moves in opposite direction |

### Failed Auction Formation

```
     |
     | Attempt to auction higher
     |    /\  <- Failed Auction (can't hold)
     |   /  \
     |--/----\---- Resistance Level
     |        \
     |         \
     |          \  Reversal continues
     v
```

### Ray Barros Revisit Rule

> "After a failed auction, the market will revisit the failed auction zone in about 5-6 days in over 75% of the cases."

**Trading Implication:**
- Initial move is OPPOSITE to the failed direction
- Failed auction zone becomes future resistance/support
- Plan for potential revisit within 5-6 days

## Combined SFP + Failed Auction Setup

### The Power of Combination

When SFP is followed by a Failed Auction, you get:
1. **SFP** = Liquidity sweep + initial reversal signal
2. **FA** = Confirmation that buyers/sellers are exhausted
3. **Combined** = Very high probability reversal

### The Sequence

```
1. SWING FAILURE PATTERN (SFP)
   └── Price sweeps the high → traps breakout buyers
   └── Closes back below → reversal begins

2. FAILED AUCTION (FA)
   └── Price attempts to retest/re-auction higher
   └── Can't even reach the SFP high
   └── Confirms sellers are in control
   └── HIGH PROBABILITY SHORT
```

### Visual Example

```
              SFP
               /\  <- 1. Sweep high (triggers stops)
              /  \
             /    \
            /      \
-----------/--------\--------- Previous High
                     \
                      \
                       \    Failed Auction
                        \     /\  <- 2. Weaker attempt
                         \   /  \     (can't reach SFP)
                          \ /    \
                           |      \
                           |       \
                           v        \
                                     -> 3. Reversal continues
```

## Trading Rules

### Entry Rules (Tom Dante Method)

| Method | Entry Point | Confirmation |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| **Basic** | Close of SFP candle | Wick + close back in range |
| **Confirmed** | Second candle closes in range | Extra confirmation |
| **Aggressive** | As price reclaims level | Fastest entry |
| **SFP + FA** | After Failed Auction confirms | Highest probability |

**Tom Dante's Recommendation:**
> "Wait for a second confirming candlestick to develop before entering a position."

### Stop Loss Placement

| Method | Stop Location | Notes |
|--------|---------------|-------|
| **Tight** | 0.2% - 0.5% beyond wick | Best R:R, more stops |
| **Standard** | 10-20 pips beyond wick | Balanced |
| **ATR-Based** | 1.5 - 2x ATR from entry | Volatility adjusted |

**Critical:** Never place stop AT the wick level - always add buffer.

### Profit Targets

| Target | Location | Action |
|--------|----------|--------|
| **TP1** | Nearest opposite swing | Take 25-50% |
| **TP2** | POC or VWAP | Take 25% |
| **TP3** | Range boundary / next liquidity | Let rest run |

### Risk-Reward Guidelines

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Minimum R:R** | 1:2 |
| **Ideal R:R** | 1:3 or better |
| **Risk per trade** | 1-2% of account |
| **Preferred risk** | 1-1.5% of account |

## Complete Trading Playbook

### Bearish Setup (Short)

```
Step 1: Identify swing high (resistance)
Step 2: Wait for price to sweep above (SFP forms)
        - Long wick above high
        - Closes back below high
Step 3: Confirm: second candle also closes below high
Step 4: Watch for retest attempt (Failed Auction)
        - Price tries to go higher
        - Can't reach SFP high
        - Weak candles, low volume
Step 5: Enter SHORT on FA confirmation
Step 6: Stop: 0.2-0.5% above SFP wick
Step 7: Target: Previous lows (1:3 R:R minimum)
```

### Bullish Setup (Long)

```
Step 1: Identify swing low (support)
Step 2: Wait for price to sweep below (SFP forms)
        - Long wick below low
        - Closes back above low
Step 3: Confirm: second candle also closes above low
Step 4: Watch for retest attempt (Failed Auction)
        - Price tries to go lower
        - Can't reach SFP low
        - Weak candles, low volume
Step 5: Enter LONG on FA confirmation
Step 6: Stop: 0.2-0.5% below SFP wick
Step 7: Target: Previous highs (1:3 R:R minimum)
```

## SFP Filters for Higher Probability

### High Probability SFP Checklist

- [ ] **HTF Alignment** - SFP direction matches HTF trend
- [ ] **Zone** - Bearish SFP in premium / Bullish SFP in discount
- [ ] **Clean Wick** - Wick is 2-3x body size
- [ ] **Strong Close** - Closes firmly back in range
- [ ] **Volume Spike** - Volume on sweep candle
- [ ] **Failed Auction** - Subsequent retest fails
- [ ] **Time** - During killzone (London/NY AM)
- [ ] **No News** - No major economic events

**Scoring:**
- 7-8 checked = A+ Setup (full size)
- 5-6 checked = B Setup (reduce size)
- <5 checked = Skip

### Double Top/Bottom Pattern

> "If an SFP forms within a Double Top or Double Bottom pattern, there is a higher chance of a reversal."

When SFP occurs at the second touch of a double pattern:
- Already established resistance/support
- More stops accumulated at level
- Higher probability sweep will fail

## SFP vs Other Concepts

### SFP = Liquidity Sweep (ICT)

| Term | Same Concept | Used By |
|------|--------------|---------|
| Swing Failure Pattern | = Liquidity Sweep | Tom Dante |
| Liquidity Sweep | = Stop Hunt | ICT |
| Stop Hunt | = Fake Breakout | Retail |

All describe the same phenomenon: price triggers stops beyond a level then reverses.

### SFP vs Unicorn Model

| Aspect | SFP | Unicorn |
|--------|-----|---------|
| **Core** | Sweep + reversal | Breaker + FVG overlap |
| **Entry** | On SFP close | On retest of overlap zone |
| **Confirmation** | Failed Auction | FVG holds |
| **Combine** | SFP creates the sweep for Unicorn setup |

## Failed Auction Trading Strategy

### Market Profile Approach

**Entry:** Trade opposite to the failed auction after confirmation
**Stop:** Just beyond the failed extreme (tight risk)
**Target:** POC or opposite Value Area extreme

### Time-Based Rules

| After FA | Expected Move |
|----------|---------------|
| Immediate | Move opposite to failed direction |
| 5-6 days | Expect revisit of FA zone (75%+ probability) |

### Integration with SMC

Failed Auction confirms:
- No institutional interest in that direction
- Distribution/Accumulation complete
- True directional move about to begin
