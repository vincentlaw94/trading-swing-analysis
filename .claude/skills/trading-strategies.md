## Market Structure

### Three Things Price Can Do

> "Price can only do three things. No matter the timeframe, it's either going up, it's going down, or going sideways. That's all it can do."

### The Three-Candle Swing System

**Swing High:** A candle with a lower high candle on each side
```
    /\
   /  \
  /    \
 Candle → Higher Candle → Lower Candle
```

**Swing Low:** A candle with a higher low candle on each side
```
 \    /
  \  /
   \/
 Candle → Lower Candle → Higher Candle
```

### Market Structure Break (MSB) = Continuation

- Uptrend: Price **closes** above the previous swing high
- Downtrend: Price **closes** below the previous swing low
- **Key Point:** Must be a CLOSE through the level, not just a wick

### Market Structure Shift (MSS) = Potential Reversal

- Uptrend breaking: Price closes below the most recent higher low
- Downtrend breaking: Price closes above the most recent lower high
- **Key Quote:** "MSS is reversal. Not always, but potentially a reversal."

### High Time Frame + Low Time Frame Alignment

> "The moment in time where the high time frame trend and the low time frame trend sync back up - that's where we take our entry."

**The Core Principle:**
1. HTF determines bias (Daily/Weekly)
2. LTF provides entry (H4/H1)
3. Wait for LTF to **shift back in alignment** with HTF
4. That moment of sync = best trade opportunity

**Example from video:**
- Daily is bearish (making lower highs, lower lows)
- H4 puts in a short-term uptrend (counter-trend bounce)
- When H4 finally breaks structure down (aligns with Daily)
- "It takes weeks to go from 80k to 97k, and then it takes 5 days to go from 97k to 60k in basically a straight line"

### Key Takeaways

1. **Price can only do three things:** Up, Down, or Sideways
2. **MSB = Continuation** (trade with trend)
3. **MSS = Potential Reversal** (alert for trend change)
4. **HTF leads LTF** - wait for congruence for best trades
5. **If it's not obvious, you're probably in a range**

## Liquidity

### What is Liquidity?

> "How I want you to think of liquidity for the purposes of these videos is resting orders. Specifically retail stop losses."

### Two Types of Liquidity

| Type | Location | Contains | Smart Money Action |
|------|----------|----------|-------------------|
| **Buy-Side (BSL)** | Above highs | Short stop losses, breakout longs | Sell into it |
| **Sell-Side (SSL)** | Below lows | Long stop losses, breakdown shorts | Buy into it |

### The Ping-Pong Effect

> "All the market does is move from buy-side liquidity taking it out, then it moves to sell-side liquidity. Back and forth, back and forth. That's all the market does."

### Why Stop Hunts Happen

> "They need the liquidity to fill their positions. Most importantly, they want to fill them at a good price."

**The Process:**
1. Price builds an obvious pattern (trend, range, support/resistance)
2. Retail traders place stops at "obvious" levels
3. Market engineers enough liquidity
4. Smart money drives price INTO the stops
5. Smart money fills their positions against those stops
6. Actual move begins

### Engineering Liquidity

> "If it's obvious, you're not the only one seeing it. Good chance it's going to get hunted."

**Common patterns that engineer liquidity:**
- Equal highs/lows (obvious stop clusters)
- Trendlines that "work every time"
- Support/resistance that "always holds"
- Each touch adds more stops behind it

### Two Types of Liquidity Sweeps

#### 1. Swing Failure Pattern (SFP)
- Wick above/below the level
- Close back on the other side
- Followed by displacement

```
SFP Example:
        /\  ← Wick above high
       /  \
      /    \
     /      \
────────────── Previous High
     │
     │ Displacement
     ↓
```

#### 2. Break and Close
- Price breaks AND closes through the level
- Then immediately reverses and closes back
- Followed by displacement

```
Break and Close Example:
     │
─────┼────── Level
     │  ← Breaks and closes above
     │
─────┼────── Level
     │  ← Closes back below
     ↓ Displacement
```

### The Confirmation: Displacement

> "Displacement is our clue that the big money, the elephant has jumped into the pool, big ripple."

**Without displacement after the sweep = could be a real breakout**
**With displacement after the sweep = confirmed liquidity sweep**

### Time Frame Context

- HTF liquidity sweeps > LTF liquidity sweeps
- A 4H bearish sweep doesn't override a bullish Weekly trend
- "The high time frame trend always dominates"

### Key Takeaways

1. **Liquidity = Resting orders (retail stops)**
2. **Market moves from liquidity to liquidity**
3. **If it's obvious, it's probably going to get hunted**
4. **Sweep types:** SFP (wick) or Break & Close
5. **Displacement confirms** the sweep was real
6. **Where you put your stop now = your entry later**

## Fair Value Gap (FVG)

### What is a Fair Value Gap?

> "A three-candle pattern where price traded through very quickly in one single direction, leaving an inefficiency."

### FVG Formation

**Bullish FVG:**
- High of candle 1 → Low of candle 3 = The Gap
- Middle candle (displacement) must be GREEN

```
     │█│ Candle 3
     │█│
     └─┘
═════════ GAP (Entry Zone)
┌─┐
│█│ Candle 1
│█│
```

**Bearish FVG:**
- Low of candle 1 → High of candle 3 = The Gap
- Middle candle (displacement) must be RED

```
│█│
│█│ Candle 1
└─┘
═════════ GAP (Entry Zone)
     ┌─┐
     │█│ Candle 3
     │█│
```

### Why FVGs Work

> "Think of it like an elastic band being stretched out very quickly and then it snaps back. That is what the fair value gap is."

**The theory:**
- Markets seek efficiency
- One-way price action = inefficiency
- Market will "fill" the gap to restore balance
- Filling the gap = your entry

### NOT All FVGs Are Equal

> "Not all fair value gaps get filled. They're not all created equal. They require context."

**High Probability FVG:**
| Factor | Bullish FVG | Bearish FVG |
|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Zone | In discount (below 50%) | In premium (above 50%) |
| Liquidity | After taking sell-side | After taking buy-side |
| HTF Trend | Aligned bullish | Aligned bearish |
| Displacement | BIG middle candle | BIG middle candle |

**Low Probability FVG:**
- In wrong zone (bullish FVG in premium)
- Against HTF trend
- Small displacement candle
- No recent liquidity sweep

### How to Trade the FVG

#### Entry Options

**Enter at FVG boundary OR spray orders in the 50% zone:**
- Bullish FVG: Enter at FVG top or upper 50%
- Bearish FVG: Enter at FVG bottom or lower 50%

#### Stop Loss Options

| Option | Location | Risk/Reward |
|--------|----------|-------------|
| **Tight** | Just beyond FVG edge | Best R:R, more stops |
| **Safe** | Beyond candle 1 | Worse R:R, fewer stops |

**Key Rule:** "If this fair value gap is good, we should not come through it at all. It should completely support price."

#### Target

- External range liquidity (swing high/low)
- Price goes from **internal** (FVG) to **external** (liquidity)

### Complete FVG Trade Framework

1. **Determine structure** - Bullish or bearish HTF
2. **Mark dealing range** - Premium and discount zones
3. **Identify liquidity** - Where are the targets?
4. **Wait for price in correct zone** - Discount for longs, Premium for shorts
5. **Zoom to LTF** - Look for FVG formation
6. **Enter at FVG** - 50% or boundary
7. **Stop beyond FVG** - Or beyond candle 1
8. **Target liquidity** - External range

### Key Takeaways

1. **FVG = 3 candles, middle is displacement**
2. **Entry = 50% of FVG** (upper for bullish, lower for bearish)
3. **Stop = Beyond FVG or beyond candle 1**
4. **Target = External range liquidity**
5. **Context is everything** - Zone, HTF trend, recent liquidity sweep
6. **Bigger displacement = higher probability**

## Dealing Ranges

### What is a Dealing Range?

> "On any chart, at any moment in time, price is within a range, and it's doing one of two things. It's either trading towards the liquidity above the range high, or towards the liquidity below the range low."

### Components of a Dealing Range

1. **Significant Swing High** - Highest point before meaningful pullback
2. **Significant Swing Low** - Lowest point before meaningful bounce
3. **50% / Equilibrium** - The midpoint (use Fib tool)
4. **Premium Zone** - Above 50% (sell zone)
5. **Discount Zone** - Below 50% (buy zone)
6. **Buy-Side Liquidity** - Above range high
7. **Sell-Side Liquidity** - Below range low

### The "Significant" Rule

> "If you can't tell, it's probably not significant."

- Use the **most recent** swing high/low
- Must be **visually obvious**
- Swing High = Highest point before meaningful pullback
- Swing Low = Lowest point before meaningful bounce

### Premium vs Discount

| Zone | Action | Logic |
|------|--------|-------|
| **Premium** (>50%) | SELL | Sell expensive |
| **Discount** (<50%) | BUY | Buy cheap |

> "We're smart money. I want to buy low, I want to sell high."

### External vs Internal Liquidity

| Type | Location | Use |
|------|----------|-----|
| **External** | Swing High/Low of range | TARGETS |
| **Internal** | FVGs, OBs within range | ENTRIES |

> "Internal range liquidity is for entries. External range liquidity is for targets."

**The Cycle:**
- Enter at internal liquidity (discount)
- Target external liquidity (swing high)
- When external is taken, look for new internal
- Ping-pong: Internal → External → Internal → External

### Russian Doll Timeframes

> "Within your weekly, there's daily. Within daily, there's hourly."

**Best Trade Setup:**
- Weekly in discount
- Daily in discount
- Hourly in discount
- All three aligned = HIGHEST probability

### Dealing Range Resets

- Each MSB creates a NEW dealing range
- Range High to Range Low = your new range
- Mark 50%, premium, discount
- Repeat the process

## Order Blocks & Breakers

### What is an Order Block?

> "The last candle or candles in the opposite direction before a large displacement move."

**Bullish OB:** Last DOWN candle before big UP move
**Bearish OB:** Last UP candle before big DOWN move

### Why Order Blocks Work

> "It's like a fingerprint that the institution has left in the chart for you."

1. Institution fills orders at OB
2. Displacement move occurs (price moves away)
3. Orders still resting at OB
4. Price returns → Orders get filled again
5. Plus: Retail puking out = more liquidity

### High Probability Order Block

| Factor | Criteria |
|--------|----------|
| **Displacement** | BIG move away (FVG present) |
| **MSB** | Creates market structure break |
| **Liquidity** | Takes out liquidity first |
| **Size** | Bigger OB candle = better |

> "Takes out liquidity, has a market structure break, big candles, massive displacement - high probability order block."

### Order Block Entry

**Bullish OB:**
- Entry: Top of OB down to 50% (mean threshold)
- Stop: Below OB (with breathing room)
- Target: External range liquidity (buy-side)

**Bearish OB:**
- Entry: Bottom of OB up to 50%
- Stop: Above OB (with breathing room)
- Target: External range liquidity (sell-side)

> "If it's a really good order block, it can trade down here, but it shouldn't close below that 50% mark."

### What is a Breaker Block?

> "A breaker is just a type of order block. It's a FAILED order block."

**Bullish Breaker:** Failed Bearish OB (resistance → support)
**Bearish Breaker:** Failed Bullish OB (support → resistance)

> "A breaker is just an SR flip. That's it."

### When Breakers Occur

> "Breakers are generally signs of REVERSAL. That's where you're most commonly going to find them - at the moment a downtrend becomes an uptrend."

**The Pattern:**
1. Order block forms (supports/resists price)
2. Price BLOWS THROUGH the OB (fails)
3. OB becomes Breaker (SR flip)
4. Price retests from other side
5. Trade the reversal

### Order Block vs Breaker

| | Order Block | Breaker |
|---|-------------|---------|
| **Trend** | With trend | At reversals |
| **What is it** | Demand/Supply zone | SR Flip |
| **When** | Continuation | MSS / Change |

## Power of Three (AMD)

### What is AMD?

> "Anytime price is about to make a move up or down, it makes a fake out in the opposite direction before the real move unfolds."

| Phase | Definition | What's Happening |
|-------|------------|------------------|
| **A**ccumulation | Sideways range | Building liquidity on both sides |
| **M**anipulation | Fake breakout (Judas Swing) | Sweeping stops, trapping traders |
| **D**istribution | Real move | The actual trend direction |

### Judas Swing

> "ICT is a big Bible guy. Judas betrayed Jesus. The Judas swing is betraying the actual direction of the move."

**Bullish AMD:**
- Accumulation: Asian range (sideways)
- Manipulation: Breakdown (sweep SSL)
- Displacement back above range confirms it
- Distribution: Rally higher

**Bearish AMD:**
- Accumulation: Asian range (sideways)
- Manipulation: Breakout (sweep BSL)
- Displacement back below range confirms it
- Distribution: Sell off lower

### Confirmation = Displacement

> "How do we know this isn't a breakout and that it's actually just manipulation? Well, I want to see displacement back into the accumulation range."

### How to Determine Direction

> "Go back to your high time frame bias. Are we bullish or bearish on the high time frame?"

If HTF is bullish:
- Breakdown of accumulation = probably manipulation
- Expect Judas swing down, then distribution UP

If HTF is bearish:
- Breakout of accumulation = probably manipulation
- Expect Judas swing up, then distribution DOWN

### Every Candle is a Power of 3

> "Almost every single candle is a power of three. Every candle, no matter what the time frame, is its own AMD cycle."

**Bullish Candle (OLHC):**
- Open → Low (manipulation)
- Low → High (distribution)
- High → Close

**Bearish Candle (OHLC):**
- Open → High (manipulation)
- High → Low (distribution)
- Low → Close

### 4 AMD Schematics to Learn

1. **Weekly AMD** - One week of trading
2. **Daily AMD** - One day of trading (Asian → NY)
3. **Single Candle AMD** - Any timeframe candle
4. **Visual AMD** - Chart pattern based (good for crypto)

### Daily AMD Sessions (EST)

| Session | Phase | Time |
|---------|-------|------|
| Asian | Accumulation | 7 PM - 2 AM |
| London | Manipulation (often) | 2 AM - 5 AM |
| NY AM | Distribution | 8 AM - 12 PM |

## Optimal Trade Entry (OTE)

### What is OTE?

> "OTE is really just a specific portion of a retracement. When price is retracing, say like 50%, that's a good spot to get in, but OTE is where price trades into the sweet spot - between 61.8% and 78.6%."

### The OTE Zone: Fibonacci Levels

| Level | Name | Significance |
|-------|------|--------------|
| **61.8%** | Golden Ratio | OTE zone begins |
| **70.5%** | Sweet Spot | Optimal entry (61.8 + 78.6 / 2) |
| **78.6%** | Deep Discount | OTE zone ends, invalidation nearby |

> "The sweet spot is 70.5%. That's where I want my entry. That's the middle of the OTE zone."

### Why OTE Works

1. **Deep retracement = Better price**
   - Entering at 70.5% vs 50% = more profit, tighter stop

2. **Close to invalidation**
   - Stop just below 78.6% or swing low
   - Tight risk, wide reward

3. **Where smart money re-enters**
   - Institutions add to positions at OTE
   - Your entry aligns with theirs

> "If you entered at the 50%, you've already given up 20% of the move. At OTE, you're getting in at a way better price with way less risk."

### OTE Setup Rules

**For Bullish OTE:**
1. Identify bullish structure (HTF bias up)
2. Wait for pullback into discount
3. Mark swing high and swing low
4. Apply Fib from swing low → swing high
5. Entry zone: 61.8% - 78.6%
6. Limit order at 70.5% (sweet spot)
7. Stop loss: Below swing low (with buffer)
8. Target: Previous high or next BSL

**For Bearish OTE:**
1. Identify bearish structure (HTF bias down)
2. Wait for pullback into premium
3. Mark swing high and swing low
4. Apply Fib from swing high → swing low
5. Entry zone: 61.8% - 78.6%
6. Limit order at 70.5% (sweet spot)
7. Stop loss: Above swing high (with buffer)
8. Target: Previous low or next SSL

### Combining OTE with PD Arrays

> "OTE by itself is good. OTE with an order block is better. OTE with an order block AND a fair value gap? That's the holy grail."

**Highest Probability Setup:**

**Stacking Confluences:**

### OTE Invalidation

> "If price closes below the 78.6% on a bullish setup, that's not a retracement anymore. That's breaking structure."

**Invalidation Rules:**
- **Bullish:** Close below 78.6% = structure break likely
- **Bearish:** Close above 78.6% = structure break likely

## Order Block vs Fair Value Gap

### When to Use Each

> "I get asked this all the time - should I enter at the order block or the fair value gap? The answer is: it depends on what the market is giving you."

### Order Block Characteristics

| Aspect | Order Block |
|--------|-------------|
| **What it is** | Last opposing candle before displacement |
| **Why it works** | Institutional orders resting there |
| **Best for** | Clear displacement, obvious candle |
| **Fill rate** | Lower (price may not reach) |
| **R:R** | Better (deeper entry) |

### FVG Characteristics

| Aspect | Fair Value Gap |
|--------|----------------|
| **What it is** | Gap between candle 1 high and candle 3 low |
| **Why it works** | Price fills inefficiency |
| **Best for** | When you want higher fill probability |
| **Fill rate** | Higher (closer to current price) |
| **R:R** | Slightly worse (not as deep) |

### The Spectrum of Entry

```
Current Price
    ↓
═══════════════ FVG Top (shallowest entry)
═══════════════ FVG 50% (CE)
═══════════════ FVG Bottom
═══════════════ OB Top
═══════════════ OB 50%
═══════════════ OB Bottom (deepest entry)
    ↓
Swing Low (stop loss)
```

> "The deeper you go, the better the R:R, but the lower your fill probability."

### Decision Framework

**Use FVG when:**
- You want higher probability of fill
- FVG is in the right zone (discount/premium)
- Momentum is strong, shallow pullback expected
- Time-sensitive (need to be in the trade)

**Use Order Block when:**
- You want better R:R
- OB is in OTE zone (61.8-78.6%)
- Market is slower, deeper pullback expected
- Willing to wait for fill

**Use BOTH when:**
- FVG overlaps with OB (ideal scenario)
- Enter at FVG 50%, add at OB
- Or wait for OB if FVG doesn't hold

### Layering Orders

> "I like to spray my orders. Some at the FVG, some at the OB. That way I'm always getting some fill."

**Example Long Setup:**
- 25% at FVG top
- 25% at FVG 50% (CE)
- 25% at OB top
- 25% at OB 50%

Average entry somewhere in the middle, guaranteed fill if price reaches any level.

### Key Takeaways

1. **FVG = Higher fill probability, shallower entry**
2. **OB = Better R:R, deeper entry**
3. **Best case:** FVG and OB overlap
4. **Spray orders** across both levels
5. **Context determines choice** - zone, momentum, HTF bias

### BPR - Balanced Price Range

A BPR forms when a **bullish FVG and bearish FVG overlap** at the same price level.

```

         │═════════│ ← OVERLAP = BPR (Highest Probability Zone)

FVG      │  FVG    │
         └─────────┘
```

**Why BPR is powerful:**
- Double layer of institutional imbalance
- Two opposing forces at one price = magnetic zone
- Highest probability entry in entire PD Array framework

**Trading BPR:**
1. Wait for BPR to form (FVG overlap)
2. Let price move away from zone
3. Enter on retest of BPR zone
4. Stop beyond the BPR
5. Target: External liquidity

### ICT Swing Point Hierarchy

Price swings are classified in a hierarchy from short-term to long-term:

| Level | Name | Formation Rule |
|-------|------|----------------|
| **STH/STL** | Short-Term High/Low | 3-candle swing (basic) |
| **ITH/ITL** | Intermediate-Term High/Low | STH/STL with STH/STL on both sides |
| **LTH/LTL** | Long-Term High/Low | ITH/ITL with ITH/ITL on both sides |

```
Hierarchy: STH → ITH → LTH
           STL → ITL → LTL

Each tier requires two same-tier neighbours that are lower (for highs) or higher (for lows).
```

**Liquidity at Swing Points:**
- **STH/STL** = Internal/short-term liquidity targets
- **ITH/ITL** = Intermediate draw on liquidity
- **LTH/LTL** = Major external liquidity (big moves)

### Advanced SMC Concepts

#### Inversion Fair Value Gap (IFVG)

When an FVG gets invalidated, it becomes an inverted zone:
- **Bearish FVG** broken upward → becomes **support**
- **Bullish FVG** broken downward → becomes **resistance**

This follows SR flip logic. The IFVG can be used as an entry on a retest.

#### Mitigation Block

A mitigation block forms when price returns to a previous order block to rebalance unfilled institutional orders:
- Price creates OB → moves away
- Price returns → "mitigates" unfilled orders
- Often occurs before continuation

**Mitigation Status:**
| Status | Meaning | Trade Action |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| **Unmitigated** | Price hasn't returned | Fresh zone, highest probability |
| **Partially Mitigated** | Price tapped but didn't fill | Still valid, lower probability |
| **Fully Mitigated** | Price filled the zone | Zone used up, look for new |

#### Consequent Encroachment (CE)

The 50% level of any PD array:
- FVG 50% = CE of the gap
- OB 50% = Mean threshold
- Swing 50% = Equilibrium

**Entry Precision:**
- Enter at CE for best R:R
- Price should reject from CE
- If price closes through CE, zone may fail
